The home page is rendering successfully with the chosen editorial naturalism direction. The typography, white space, and navigation hierarchy are working well at first glance. The uploaded Donnie Bats cover displays correctly, and the generated prairie hero image is loading as intended. The school visit photo also displays correctly in the educator section.

## Additional preview findings

The Books page is visually strong, but the current structure creates a duplicate page-level heading. The main hero statement should remain the only H1 on that route, while the featured Donnie Bats title should be reduced to an H2 for cleaner document structure and SEO.

## School Visits and About review

The School Visits page is working well visually. The hero image is strong and the page feels credible for educators. The booking form is present and easy to scan. The About page also reads clearly, but the long first-person narrative may benefit from slightly more vertical rhythm or segmentation in a later polish pass if space permits.

The current missing-photo placeholders are visually consistent with the design system and do not feel broken. They read as intentional reserved spaces for future author portraits, testimonials, and downloadable collateral.

## Photography and Contact review

The Photography page is functioning well and the gallery-led presentation feels premium without drifting into a generic portrait portfolio. The pricing cards and ownership message are clear. The Contact page is also visually coherent, though its extracted article text is sparse because the structure is highly visual rather than text-heavy. Visually, the form and direct-contact block are both present and readable.

At this stage the full route set appears to be implemented and navigable: Home, Books, School Visits, About, Photography, and Contact.
